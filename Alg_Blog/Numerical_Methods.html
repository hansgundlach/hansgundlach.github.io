Introduction to Multiplication

Basic Multiplication

In grade school you may have learned several methods of multiplication. These methods are easy to apply on small numbers but are inefficent with large numbers. Plain multiplication runs in $O(n^2)$ time because you have to compare the digits of both numbers.

Karatsuba Improvment

In 1960 Anatoly Karatusba discovered a slight improvement on the tradditional algorithm bringing multiplication complexity to $O(n^1.5)$. Karatsuba's discovery brought forth a whole new generation of multiplication algorithms. Todays fastest integer multiplication algorithm is the Shonhage-Strassen algorithmn which multiplies numbers in $O(n * lg(n) * lg(lg(n)))$ time.
